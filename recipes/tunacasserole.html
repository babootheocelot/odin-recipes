Tuna Noodle Casserole, Sipiora Style

When I was young, one of the first things I ever learned to make on the stove was Tuna Noodle Casserole, or as I called it back then, Tuna Casserole. Our Sipiora version is very different from most other true casseroles out there, as it doesn't involve baking at all.

But that's not a casserole at all, you cry.
You're right. It's not. It's still my favorite food in the world, the food I reach for every time I'm sick, want comfort food, or just feel like eating a huge bowl of warm noodles covered in non-tomato sauce. This is that recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 10oz can, Campbell's Cream of Mushroom Soup

  • 1 can albacore tuna in water, any size

  • 1/3c milk (or water if you're pressed)

  • 4 oz wide or extra wide egg noodles

    • Who am I kidding? Pick your favorite pasta, and use it! It'll soak up all the delicious sauce no matter what.

  • salt and pepper to taste

Recipe

  1. Set a quart of water to boil on the stove.
  2. Add noodles and cook according to directions.
  3. Open the cream of mushroom soup. Open and drain the albacore tuna.
  4. Mix the two in a medium bowl, breaking up larger chunks of tuna.
  5. Add milk and stir until combined.
  6. Add salt and pepper to taste.

    7. I normally add only pepper, as the cream of mushroom soup is salty enough on its own.

  7. Drain noodles and add to mixture. Stir to combine.
  8. Enjoy with your favorite veggie.

    9. Pro Tip: add the veggies directly to the mixture. Thank me later!
